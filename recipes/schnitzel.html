Wiener Schnitzel recipe

Description

Wiener Schnitzel is a traditional Austrian dish made from thinly pounded veal cutlets.
The simplicity of the ingredients highlights the quality of the veal, making it a beloved classic. Whether for a special occasion or a comforting meal, Wiener Schnitzel never fails to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 4 veal cutlets (about 150g each)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (preferably fresh)
  • 1 cup vegetable oil or clarified butter (for frying)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Cutlets:
    1. Place each veal cutlet between two sheets of plastic wrap or parchment paper.
    2. Gently pound the cutlets with a meat mallet until they are about 1/4 inch thick.
    3. Season both sides of the cutlets with salt and pepper.
  2. Set Up Breading Station:
    1. Place the flour in a shallow dish.
    2. Beat the eggs in another shallow dish.
    3. Place the breadcrumbs in a third shallow dish.
  3. Bread the Cutlets:
    1. Dredge each cutlet in the flour, shaking off any excess.
    2. Dip the cutlet into the beaten eggs, ensuring it is fully coated.
    3. Press the cutlet into the breadcrumbs, making sure it is evenly coated. Do not press the breadcrumbs into the meat too firmly.
  4. Fry the Schnitzels:
    1. Heat the oil or clarified butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Once the oil is hot (but not smoking), add the breaded cutlets. Fry them in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding the pan.
    3. Cook each cutlet for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
    4. Transfer the cooked schnitzels to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  5. Serve:
    1. Serve the Wiener Schnitzel hot with lemon wedges on the side.
    2. Traditional accompaniments include potato salad, cucumber salad, or fries.

Enjoy your homemade Wiener Schnitzel!
